Who

Persona: Jose Matias Delgado

Known as: el Padre de El Salvador

Edad: 1767-1832

Padres: Panama y Guatamalen ambos creoles (born from conquistador lines)

Pais: El Salvador

Universidad: Guatamala city @

  • Tridentine College y Seminary of Our Lady de la Assumption

  • Universidad de San Carlos

Mayor: Cannon Law and Theology (1782-1796)

Trabajo: Sacerdote – Politico - Juez

Famous porque

  • 1811 Leader in the 1st independence movement

  • 1820 Sent from Guatemala City back to San Salvador as provincial deputy

  • 1821 Becomes 2nd president after 1st one killed in battle after 3 months

  • 1823 Sent as a constituent congress to the Federal Republic of Central America (FRCA): equivalent to 1st continental congress

  • 1824 Appointed 1st Bishop of San Salvador

  • 1824 Creates 1st newspaper with public funds lasting until 1850

  • 1832 President of the National Assembly (NA) : the NA is equivalent to US Congress

  • 1833 designated by NA as “Meritorious of the Fatherland”

  • 1967 NA awards a second title as “Father of the Central American Fatherland”

Background

  • From 1500-1800s country of New Spain

  • Operated under the Caste System

  • Primarily enslavement for silver, gold mines

    • First 30 years just looting resources

  • Later moved into ranching, farming plantations

Caste System

  1. Peninsular - Iberian born

  2. Creole - Iberian parents, America born – Padre Delgado

  3. Mestizo - 1 euro + 1 indio

  4. Mulato - 1 euro + 1 african

  5. Indio - 1 indo + 1 indio

  6. Lobo - 1 indio + 1 african

  7. African - 1 african + 1 african

Domino Effect…Unfolding of the old Empire

  • 1808 Bonaparte conquers Espana. Places el hermano in charge of Spain & Colonies

  • 1808-1821 New spain Viceroy and Govener control split between old & new systems

  • 1811 The 1st Independence begins

    • Tres llaves players: Jose Delgado (brains), Manual Jose Arce (bronze), Santiago Celis (medico)

      • John Adams, George Washington, Ben Franklin/Thomas Jefferson

    • Signal church bell of La Merced, 3 units confiscate arms and funds of treasury

    • Liberty cry “There is no King, nor Intendant, nor Captain General. We only must obey our alcaldes”

  • Independence lasts month, Royal authority re-established. All parties promises to do better.

  • 1813 Delgado appointed provincial deputy Guatemala City and Seminary director.

  • 1814 2nd Independence movement.

    • Delgado - not in country

    • Arce - 4yrs jail

    • Celis - 4yrs jail and dies after release

Spain rule phase out

  • 1819 - 1821 Pedro Barriere appointed governor of El Salvador under New Spain

  • 1821 New Spain declares independence from Spain becomes 1st Mexican empire

    • Captaincy of Guatemala independence, joins Mexican empire. Includes all central America intendants

    • El Salvador declares independence from coalition. Delgado assumes presidency after Barriere KIA

  • 1823 surrender to Guatemalan forces. Mexico grants autonomous rule of central America

  • 1823 - 1824 countries become “The United Provinces of Central America”.

  • 1832 Father Delgado dies of flu (most likely)

FRCA Conflict

  • 1825 notably Arce become the FRCA 1st president

  • 1827-1829 FRCA 1st Civil War

  • 1838-1841 FRCA 2nd Civil war fractures and dissolves one country at a time

  • 1838 increase in populations result in multiple issues

    • Cholera, political, social, poverty, religious, federal funding / tax collection

    • Nicaragua first to break way

  • 1841 Civil War ends

    • Treaty affirms trade/ commerace clauses between countries
